# 🎹 Flowstate Audio - Instrument & Musical Typing Guide

## How to Use Instruments in Your Project

### Step 1: Create a MIDI Track
1. Click the **"+ MIDI"** button at the bottom of the Track List (left panel)
2. A new MIDI track will appear (purple/blue icon with piano keys)

### Step 2: Add an Instrument
1. **Select the MIDI track** by clicking on it in the Track List
2. Look at the **Inspector Panel** on the RIGHT side of the screen
3. Scroll down to the **"INSERT EFFECTS"** section
4. Click the **"+ ADD"** button
5. A plugin menu will appear showing ALL plugins including:
   - **FS-Analog** - Analog synthesizer (bass, lead, pads)
   - **FS-Sampler** - Drum machine with 16 pads
   - Plus 40+ effect plugins

### Step 3: Select Your Instrument
- Click **"FS-Analog"** for a classic analog synthesizer
- Click **"FS-Sampler"** for drums and samples
- The instrument UI will appear in the plugin rack

### Step 4: Use Musical Typing
1. **Click anywhere on the MIDI track** to make sure it's selected
2. Press keys on your **computer keyboard** to play notes:

#### Keyboard Layout (Musical Typing):
```
Lower Octave (C3-B3):
  Z X C V B N M , .
  │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │
  C D E F G A B C D

Upper Octave (C4-B4):
  A S D F G H J K L
  │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │
  C D E F G A B C D

Top Row (C5-B5):
  Q W E R T Y U I O
  │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │ │
  C D E F G A B C D
```

#### Octave Controls:
- **Z** = C3 (low octave)
- **A** = C4 (middle octave)
- **Q** = C5 (high octave)

### Step 5: Record MIDI
1. **Arm the track** by clicking the **Red circle button** in the track header
2. Click the main **RECORD button** (red circle) in the transport bar
3. Play your keyboard - notes will be recorded
4. Click **STOP** to finish recording

### Step 6: Edit MIDI Notes (Piano Roll)
1. **Double-click** on the MIDI clip in the timeline
2. The **Piano Roll** will open at the bottom
3. Click the **Piano Roll** tab if it's not visible
4. Draw, move, resize, and delete notes
5. Press **Escape** to close Piano Roll

## 🎛️ Available Instruments

### FS-Analog Synthesizer
**Best for:** Bass, leads, pads, atmospheric sounds

**Controls:**
- **2 Oscillators** with waveform selection (Sine, Square, Saw, Triangle)
- **Filter** with cutoff, resonance, and envelope
- **ADSR Envelope** for amplitude shaping
- **LFO** for modulation effects
- **Unison** for thick, detuned sounds

**Presets:** Classic Bass, Lead Synth, Warm Pad, etc.

### FS-Sampler Drum Machine
**Best for:** Drums, percussion, one-shots

**Controls:**
- **16 Pads** arranged in a 4x4 grid
- Each pad has individual:
  - Volume, Pan, Pitch
  - ADSR envelope
  - Sample loading
- **Master Volume** and **Master Pitch** controls

**Usage:**
1. Click on a pad
2. Load a sample (click the folder icon or drag & drop)
3. Play using musical typing or click the pads

## 📝 Quick Workflow

1. **Create MIDI track** → Click "+ MIDI"
2. **Select track** → Click on the track name
3. **Add instrument** → Inspector Panel (right) → "+ ADD" → Choose FS-Analog or FS-Sampler
4. **Play notes** → Use your keyboard (Z, X, C, V, etc.)
5. **Record** → Arm track (red circle) → Record button → Play keyboard
6. **Edit** → Double-click MIDI clip → Piano Roll opens

## 💡 Pro Tips

1. **Layer Instruments**: Add multiple instruments to one MIDI track for rich sounds

3. **Effects**: Add reverb, delay, or compression after the instrument in the plugin rack

5. **MIDI Controller**: Connect a MIDI keyboard for better playing experience

## ❓ Troubleshooting

**Q: I don't see the Inspector Panel**  
A: Make sure a track is selected (click on it in the Track List)

**Q: Musical typing isn't working**  
A: Make sure the MIDI track is selected and you're not typing in a text field

**Q: No sound when playing**  
A: Check that:
- The track volume is up (slider in track header)
- The track is not muted (M button)
- Master volume is up (bottom right)

**Q: Where's the Piano Roll?**  
A: Double-click any MIDI clip, or click the "Piano Roll" tab at the bottom
